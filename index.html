Kumrat
A Hidden Gem in Pakistan's Northern Landscape

Kumrat Valley, nestled in the Upper Dir district of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, is one of Pakistan’s most enchanting and unspoiled tourist destinations. Known for its lush green meadows, thick forests, and gushing streams, Kumrat Valley offers a serene retreat for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike.

Why Visit
Kumrat Valley?

Kumrat Valley’s untouched beauty and peaceful ambiance make it an ideal getaway. The valley is renowned for its: Panoramic Landscapes: Majestic snow-clad mountains and verdant plains stretch as far as the eye can see. Freshwater Streams: Streams like the Jahaz Banda Waterfall are perfect for fishing, photography, and relaxation. Camping Sites: Lush meadows and open spaces are perfect for camping under the starlit sky. Cool Climate: Even in summer, the valley provides a refreshing escape from the scorching heat.

Adventure Awaits in
Kumrat Valley

Kumrat Valley isn’t just a destination for relaxation; it’s also a playground for thrill-seekers. From hiking and trekking to fishing and rafting, there’s something for everyone. The challenging trails and high-altitude passes test your endurance while rewarding you with breathtaking views.

Black Waters

Spring

Veer into the heart of Kumrat's hidden depths and discover Kala Chashma, the Black Spring. Here, amidst the towering pines and rugged slopes , water emerges from the earth, not in a rush of white froth , but with a quiet, obsidian sheen. Dark stones cradle the crystalline flow, lending the water its enigmatic hue and whispering tales of ancient magic. Kala Chashma isn't just a spring; it's a portal to the valley's soul, a place where the shadows dance with the light and the whispers of the mountains echo in the stillness.

Dasht e Laila

Agri Land

Dasht-e-Laila, often called the "Desert of Laila," is a unique and breathtaking plateau located in the upper reaches of the Kumrat Valley in Pakistan. It's a high-altitude expanse that offers a stark yet stunning landscape, quite different from the lush greenery Kumrat is known for.

Badgoi Top

Mountain Peak Land

Badgoi Top is a high mountain pass that connects Kumrat Valley with the Swat Valley, specifically the Kalam area. It's known for its breathtaking beauty and is gaining popularity as a must-visit destination in the region.

Panjkora

River

The Panjkora River is a major river in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan. It originates in the Hindu Kush mountains and flows west into the Swat River. The Panjkora River is a popular destination for rafting and kayaking, as well as fishing and swimming.
